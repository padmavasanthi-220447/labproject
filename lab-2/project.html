In smart agriculture, audio-related applications primarily involve acoustic monitoring for tracking biodiver sity and livestock health, and using audio messages via ICT for farmer education and decision-making.Smart audio sensors equipped with AI species recognition can identify the calls of birds, pollinators, and other wildlife. This non-invasive method helps assess the health and variety of the local ecosystem, providing data for conservation and land-use decisions

Smart agriculture leverages modern technology to optimize farming practices, improve efficiency, and enhance sustainability. Key technologies include the Internet of Things (IoT) sensors, drones for field monitoring, GPS-guided systems for precision planting, and data analytics platforms that inform decisions regarding irrigation and fertilization. This approach enables farmers to manage resources more effectively, reduce waste, and increase yields while minimizing environmental impact. Ultimately, smart agriculture helps in addressing global food security challenges by making food production systems more resilient and productive.
